COVID-19 Death Forecasts: Visualizing Uncertainty Over Time

This visualization explores how forecasts of cumulative COVID-19 deaths in the United States changed over time. The solid line represents the median prediction, while the shaded region shows uncertainty (2.5th–97.5th percentile range). Use the dropdown to see how predictions evolved across forecast dates.

Understanding the Quantile Distribution

Each dot represents a predicted value at a specific quantile. Lower quantiles reflect more pessimistic forecasts, while higher quantiles reflect more optimistic ones. The median (0.5) is the central prediction used in the line chart above.

This tool is designed to help users understand how forecasted COVID-19 deaths and their associated uncertainty change over time. By comparing predictions across forecast dates, users can identify whether models become more or less certain and how estimates shift as new data became available.
